Privacy Policy

Screen Recorder

Published by Soft Standard · Effective 3 July 2026 · Applies to all versions of the Screen Recorder desktop application for Windows.

The short version: Screen Recorder runs entirely on your computer. Your recordings, audio, settings, and logs are stored locally on your own device. The application does not create an account, does not use analytics or telemetry, and does not send your recordings or any personal data to us or to any third party.

1. Who we are

Screen Recorder is a Windows desktop application developed and published by Soft Standard ("we", "us", "our"). It captures your screen, system audio, and microphone and encodes them into a standard MP4 video file saved on your computer. This policy explains what the application does and does not do with your information.

2. Information the application handles

To do its job, Screen Recorder works with the following data, all of which stays on your device:

  • Screen content — the monitor or window you choose to record is captured as video frames while a recording is active.
  • System (loopback) audio — the sound playing on your computer, if you enable it for the recording.
  • Microphone audio — captured only if you enable microphone recording and grant Windows microphone permission.
  • Application settings — your preferences such as output folder, resolution, frame rate, encoder, and selected audio devices.
  • Diagnostic logs — local text logs recording the application's own activity (e.g. which devices were used and any errors), to help you troubleshoot problems. These are technical logs, not a record of what you recorded.

We do not collect names, email addresses, contacts, location data, browsing history, or any advertising identifiers. The application has no login and no user profile.

3. Where your data is stored

Everything the application produces is written to your local computer only:

  • Recordings (MP4 files) — saved to the output folder you choose. By default this is your Windows Videos folder.
  • Settings — stored in %AppData%\ScreenRecorder\settings.json.
  • Logs — stored in %LocalAppData%\ScreenRecorder\logs\, with old log files rotated out automatically.

You are in full control of these files. You can move, back up, or delete your recordings, settings, and logs at any time using Windows.

4. What we do not do

  • We do not transmit your recordings, audio, or screen content off your device.
  • We do not use analytics, tracking, telemetry, or advertising SDKs.
  • We do not require an account, and we do not collect or store personal information on our servers — we operate no such server for the application.
  • We do not sell or share your data with anyone.

5. Permissions the application uses

Windows may ask you to grant the following, and each is used solely to provide the recording feature you requested:

  • Graphics / screen capture — to record the monitor or window you select.
  • Microphone — to record microphone audio, only when you turn that option on.
  • File system access — to save recordings, settings, and logs to the locations described above.

You can review or revoke microphone access at any time in Windows Settings › Privacy & security › Microphone.

6. Network use

The Screen Recorder application itself does not require or use an internet connection to record. It functions fully offline.

The only network activity associated with the project happens outside the application, during installation or updates: our optional install/update script and the Microsoft Store download the application package from their respective distribution servers (for example, GitHub Releases or the Microsoft Store). These transfers deliver software to you and are governed by the privacy practices of those platforms. The application does not phone home after it is installed.

7. Third-party components

Screen Recorder bundles FFmpeg to encode and package your video. FFmpeg runs locally on your machine as part of the recording pipeline and does not transmit data. Its license text is included with the installed application.

If you obtain Screen Recorder through the Microsoft Store, the Store's own privacy statement covers the download, licensing, and update process that Microsoft performs.

8. Children's privacy

Screen Recorder is a general-purpose utility and is not directed at children. Because it does not collect any personal information, it does not knowingly collect information from children.

9. Data security

Because your data never leaves your computer, its security is tied to the security of your own device and Windows user account. We recommend keeping Windows up to date and protecting your device with a password or other login.

10. Your choices and control

Since all data is local, you retain complete control. You can delete any recording, clear the settings file, or remove the log folder at any time. Uninstalling the application removes the program; you may separately delete your recordings and configuration if you wish to remove them as well.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy to reflect changes in the application or for legal and regulatory reasons. When we do, we will revise the "Effective" date above and publish the updated policy at this page. Continued use of the application after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
